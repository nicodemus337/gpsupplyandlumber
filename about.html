Roots in Loreauville

Since the 1800s, the village of Loreauville has been built on agriculture, boat‐building and community service. Early settlers cleared sugar cane fields beside Bayou Teche; townsfolk depended on locally owned stores that knew their name, their jobsite and their family. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Gonsoulin Mercantile: The First Chapter

In 1867, Adrien Gonsoulin opened a coffee house and bakery in Loreauville — soon thereafter turning it into a general mercantile store. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6} Over time, the business evolved with the community, offering tools, lumber and household goods to the mechanics, farmers and makers of the area.

Oubre & Lumber: Serving Builders Locally

As Loreauville grew, so did its building needs. Oubre Lumber Company opened a branch and served the growing demand for lumber and hardware in the region. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8} It added depth to that early “store that knew your name” ethos.

Now: Gonsoulin & Price Supply & Lumber

Today, the business operates under the name Gonsoulin & Price Supply & Lumber, continuing the tradition of a family-owned hardware and lumber yard in Loreauville. Our promise: There will always be a locally owned hardware store here. Whatever you build, fix or feed, we’re rooted here with you.

Serving Builders, Ranchers & Families

From framing lumber to coated nails, from delivery to contractor accounts, we support local builders, ranchers and generations of home-owners. Because a place like Loreauville deserves a store that knows the land and its people.
